The Consultant's Dilemma

Your clients — whether they're evaluating acquisitions, entering new markets, or managing portfolio risk — all ask the same question: "What structural constraints will force decisions?" You see the gap: financial analysis misses capital constraints. Legal review misses market exposure. Ops analysis misses governance risk. Each function sees one angle. None see the integrated picture.

Result: You either skip structural analysis or refer clients to expensive DD firms. Either way, you miss revenue and strategic depth.

What Structural Diagnostics Solves for Your Clients

  • Capital Constraints: Hidden debt covenants, liquidity risks, funding gaps that force decisions post-close.
  • Regulatory Exposure: Compliance risks, licensing dependencies, threshold events that trigger enforcement.
  • Market Vulnerability: Competitor moves, customer concentration, macro exposure that forces pivot or exit.
  • Operational Scaling: Tech debt, talent gaps, process bottlenecks that slow growth or integration.
  • Governance Risk: Board alignment, decision rights, stakeholder conflicts that paralyze execution.
  • Deal Timing: When constraints become acute. What window exists before optionality is gone.
